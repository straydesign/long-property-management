Maintenance Rules & Standards

How we handle maintenance, repairs, and seasonal services. Clear thresholds, documented work, and defined emergencies.

How maintenance requests flow

Every request follows the same documented process, whether it's a small fix or a larger project.

1

Request received

Issue is identified during a property check or reported by the owner. We triage within the same business day.

2

Assess and classify

We determine scope, estimated cost, and whether it falls under or over your approval threshold. Emergencies are flagged immediately.

3

Approval (when required)

Under your threshold: we proceed and notify. Over your threshold: we send scope and estimate, then wait for your go-ahead. Emergencies: we act to prevent damage.

4

Execute and document

Work is completed, photographed, and documented with itemized notes. You receive a report with photos and invoice.

5

Close and file

Invoice is filed in your owner records. You have a complete history of every repair and its cost.

Approval threshold rules

You set the dollar limit. We follow it.

Under your threshold

We proceed to keep the unit safe and functional. You receive notification with photo documentation and an itemized invoice after the work is done.

Over your threshold

We contact you with a scope description and estimated cost. Work begins only after you approve. No exceptions (other than emergencies).

Emergency authorization

Emergencies are authorized to prevent property damage or safety risk. We act first, then document and report immediately. Your safety and your property come first.

Threshold is yours to set

Common thresholds range from $250 to $500. During onboarding, we help you pick a number that balances responsiveness with oversight.

What counts as an emergency

Emergencies are narrowly defined. This list sets expectations for both you and us.

Emergency situations

  • No heat in winter — Risk of frozen pipes and habitability violation
  • Active water leak — Risk of structural damage and mold
  • Electrical hazard — Exposed wiring, sparking, burning smell
  • Sewage backup — Health hazard requiring immediate response
  • Broken exterior door or lock — Security compromise

Not emergencies (handled normally)

  • — Running toilet (non-overflowing)
  • — Appliance malfunction (fridge, dishwasher, etc.)
  • — Cosmetic damage or normal wear
  • — Landscaping requests outside scope
  • — Pest control (unless infestation is hazardous)

Documentation standards

Every job creates a paper trail you can reference.

Photo proof

Before and after photos when applicable. You see exactly what was done.

Itemized invoices

Labor and materials listed separately. No lump sums, no ambiguity.

Written notes

What was found, what was done, and any recommendations for future attention.

Seasonal maintenance approach

Erie weather demands proactive care. Here's how seasonal services fit the maintenance system.

Winter: snow + ice

  • Accumulation triggers defined at onboarding
  • Response window after snowfall event
  • Driveways, walkways, and access points cleared
  • Ice management as conditions require
  • Documentation per event

Spring/Summer/Fall: lawn + exterior

  • Mowing on a regular seasonal schedule
  • Basic exterior upkeep and cleanliness
  • Scope defined upfront — no surprise add-ons
  • Adjustments by request only
  • Property appearance maintained for occupancy
